Model + methodology

The Banister Impulse-Response Model

Developed by Eric Banister, the impulse-response model treats each training session as a physiological impulse with two competing outcomes: durable fitness gain and short-lived fatigue cost.

Maestro extends that model with ACWR, monotony, and short-window spike/strain diagnostics so daily coaching recommendations are tied to both adaptation and risk management.

Fitness (CTL) — 42-day EMA of daily TRIMP.

Fatigue (ATL) — 7-day EMA of daily TRIMP.

Readiness (TSB) — CTL minus ATL.

Load Risk (ACWR) — ATL divided by CTL.

10-day spike & strain — short-term overload and accumulated stress signal.

TRIMP = duration × %HRR × zone-weight  |  CTLₜ = CTLₜ₋₁ + (TRIMP − CTLₜ₋₁) / 42  |  TSB = CTL − ATL  |  ACWR = ATL / CTL
